Major Cities:

Pyongyang

The capital city of the DPRK, Pyongyang conveys a brutalist sense of leadership and is home of the Arch of Triumph, Mansudae Fountain Park, Mansudae Grand Monument.

Kaesong

The 160km long Reunification Highway directly connects Pyongyang and Kaesong. It is the closest city to the Panmunjom Armistice Village and the DMZ.

Pyongsong

The location of Kim Jong-suk Higher Middle School, where the DPRK's most talented students study. It is named after Kim Jong-il's first wife, an anti-japanese guerilla activist.

Sinuiju

The border town of Sinuiju faces the Chinese city of Dandong. The two cities are separated by international border of the Yalu River.

Visit

The Democratic People's Republic of Korea has been accepting tourists since 1993.

Visiting the Hermit Kingdom is an culturally enriching experience, opening your eyes to breadth of human civilization and experience..

Some FAQs


    Is it safe to travel to the DPRK?
      Yes. You will be legally entering the country as a tourist therefore you must obey the local laws to ensure your own safety. Check out your own government's advice on travelling to North Korea. For example, here is the British Government's latest advice.

    Can an American citizen travel to North Korea?
      Unfortunately due to legislation put in place by the US Government, effective as of the first of September, 2017, American citizens are not able to travel to North Korea.

    Will travelling to North Korea affect me travelling to other countries?
      Not at all. No countries will refuse you entry because you have travelled to North Korea - including the USA and South Korea.

    Will I be spied on while I'm there?
      Despite claims made on some blogs and newspapers, it is very unlikely that westerners will be spied on.
